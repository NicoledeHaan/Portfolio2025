Linked files

MediaLab, a file-sharing platform, often received questions from users about whether they could manage or link subtitles and translations to their existing files. To address this, I designed a feature that allows users to easily link and manage subtitles and translations directly in their media library, providing a more efficient and user-friendly experience for handling multilingual content.

Service

Prototyping, UX Design, UI Design

Client

MediaLab

Tools

Adobe XD

How can MediaLab enable users to manage and link subtitles and translations to their existing files so that they can organize multilingual content?

Upload multiple languages

You can either upload files directly from your computer or link existing files from your MediaLab environment, making it possible to easily manage and organize multilingual content within your projects.

See underlying files

This allows users to easily view the underlying files of a video, like subtitles and translations. This makes it simple to check and manage multilingual content directly within your MediaLab environment.

Visually organized

Small notifications, always visible at the bottom left of the screen, provide real-time updates on active uploads, push queue and video queue. Users can hide these notifications by preference.
